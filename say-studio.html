Project Overview
Background
Say Studio was a content management platform that supported the app SAY. Before I joined the Radiant team, the front-end developer had been developing the concept of Say Studio. However, the Creative Team team needed a simple user experience that would allow them to create packs, add posts, and schedule posts for automatic publishing. Radiant understood how crucial the user experience was and hired me to take charge of their efforts.

Getting Started
I started this project by first having thorough conversation with the team about what exactly what our goals and vision were, how to we would achieve them, and most importantly understand what our users expected. I believe it is important to fully understand what the product is before diving in.
Initial Analysis
The Current Experience
Our process started with a live walkthrough to understand the current functionality and experience of Say Studio. We noticed the flow had usability problems that made it challenging for users to achieve tasks quickly and accurately. We also identified visually the user interface was distracting which made it difficult to focus on specific features. Below are are some major usability issues we found:
User Experience Issues
Add Pack and Post
One the major usability issues we came across was adding a pack or post. As you can see on the left, I sketched out the user flow to understand where to proceed.
Edit Pack and Post
Another task flow that was repetitive was editing a pack and post. A user had to go through two pages just to edit their pack or post.
Messaging States
There was no way of knowing if your pack or post had been successfully published. Another similar case scenario was if the image size was incorrect, there would be no error message until the pack was submitted. Also observed there were no loading or hover states, which could cause a user to press a CTA several times and duplicate packs or posts.
Visual Design Issues
Grid & List View
At the top, the user has the option to see the badges in a list or grid view. The grid view was difficult to skim through because some of the badge's names where longer and not fully displayed. As for the list view, the artist name at the top right corner made the badge box crowded.
Typography White Noise
Due to the excessive all-caps, bold/light fonts the text hierarchy was not clear which made the content difficult to read.
Button Inconsistencies
All the button styles are arounded, except on a few screens there were random rectangular gradients that acted as a button.
