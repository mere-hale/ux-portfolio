Project Overview
Problem
The MyEWU Portal does not allow students to have access to their account, financial aid, register for classes, and view holds and alerts. All of this information is crucial for students to be successful.
Solution
The MyEWU Portal concept app provides students with a quick and easy way to view importantinformation relevant to their education, register for classes, view holds and alerts, make payments, view account charges, and take care of financial aid!

Research
Talking to Students
As a student myself I understood the frustration of not being able to accomplish much on the app. Instead of assuming what students would like to see, I took the initiative to interveiw other students. Doing so I treated the interview as a converstation to understand what their goals and needs were and uncover painpoints.
Affinity Mapping
Determining the Pain Points
Step 1:To organize my interview insights, I used post-it notes to represent the user's pain points and used different colors for each person I talked to.
Step 2:Then I used affinity mapping to categorize all the common painpoints.
Personas
Meet Bonnie and Kyle
Based off the affinity map data from talking to students I created two personas that reflected their goals, needs, and painpoints.
The difference between both of these users is Bonnie is a freshman and is not sure what she wants to major in so class times and days don't matter to her. Versus Kyle who is a junior and believes a schedule planner would help him figure what classes he needs to take in order to graduate on time.

Information Architecture
My EWU Portal Dashboard User Flow
I had already established that a bottom toolbar would be the best type of navigation. This would include the dashboard, academic calendar, campus maps, and notfications. Since the timeline was three months my main focus was to create the My EWU Portal Dashboard. Before sketching out what the My EWU Portal Dashboard should look like, I first created a user flow of how the student would interact with the dashboard.
Ideation
White Board Sketches
Now that I had a user flow for the dashboard, my next step was to sketch out as many ideas of where are the essential features would be placed. After many iterations, the last sketch was the strongest solution to my user's needs and painpoints.
Evaluate
The Final Design
After doing a low fedlity test with the students I talked to, they were pretty happy with the final design. However there are still other features I would like to add to this application in the future. I hope to design confirmation and declined pop screens and establish how students would get notifications for important dates.
